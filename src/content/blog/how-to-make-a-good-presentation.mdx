<Message>
  <Heading>Less Text</Heading>

- More text is always a red flag.
- It's hard for people to go through entire content.
- Try to keep your content short and concise.

**Bonus**

- Keep 50 - 70 characters max per line.
- Don't extend content width till the end of presentation slide.

</Message>

<Message>
  <Heading>Color Palette</Heading>

- Stick to a single color palette for your entire presentation.
- This bring a uniform look for every slide.
- It'll contribute to Visual Hierarchy and increases Readability.

**Bonus**

Use this website for color-palette inspiration

- [Colorhunt](https://colorhunt.co/)
- [Coolors](https://coolors.co/)

</Message>

<Message>
  <Heading>Layout</Heading>

- Text alignment makes your content look more professional.
- Stick to same alignment, go for **Left-Alignment**.
- During my 10th class exams, my school Head Minsters gave advice. For scoring marks in exam, **Align your text** it makes your content look good

</Message>

<Message>
<Heading>Images & Graphics</Heading>

- Include images and graphics in presentation as they're easy to understand.
- This can help in conveying message in shorter time.

**Bonus**

Use this website for image resources

- [Unsplash](https://unsplash.com)
- [Pexels](https://pexels.com)
- [Freepik](https://freepik.com)

</Message>

<Message>
<Heading>Fonts</Heading>

- Stick to using 2 Fonts
  - Heading Font
  - Body Font
- You can use `Serif`, `Sans-Serif` fonts combination for rich contrast
- Go for more readable fonts example: monospace

**Bonus**

Here're few resources for fonts

- [Google Fonts](https://fonts.google.com/)
- [Dafont](https://www.dafont.com/)
- [Fontshare](https://www.fontshare.com/)

</Message>

<Message character="chimtu" direction="right">
  <Heading>Practice</Heading>

Practice presenting your content multiple times, This adds up confidence and even makes presentation more engaging.

</Message>
